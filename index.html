Is It Safe to Buy a Car Key Replacement Online?

Car key replacement is an integral service in the world of auto locksmith. With keyless entry and push-button start systems now the standard, regular car keys are becoming a thing of the past. However, with high costs and long waiting times, finding a quick and affordable key fob replacement service is often tricky. In addition, finding high-quality service at low cost can be challenging when demand pushes prices beyond what customers expect.


Have you lost a key or need a spare for a family member? Buying a car key replacement online is usually the best way to avoid the expensive cost. But there are a few things you will want to get in order before you buy your key. Here are a few things that you should think about and consider.

Is It Safe to Buy a Car Key Replacement Online?


Many Online Car Key Replacement Vendors Are Not Auto Locksmiths

Automotive locksmiths must know if the key they are selling will work before they sell it. Any reputable locksmith worth their salt would never hand you a new key for your lock without testing it first. The keys sold by legitimate locksmiths come from authorized dealers and suppliers, ensuring compatibility with your vehicle. Unfortunately, many aftermarket vendors usually buy keys in bulk and resell them for a quick profit.


Our pro tip: Avoid locating a locksmith or automotive security professional through Google or Bing. Instead, use vetted associations and membership groups when looking for locksmiths, such as FindALocksmith.com, Keypro.com, and PopALock.com. They are excellent resources for locating a reputable technician and avoiding locksmith scams.

You May Spend More Money on an Aftermarket Car Key Replacement

Off-brand key providers commonly claim to sell products the same as OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer). However, they are rarely backed up by the rigorous testing and quality controls that go into making genuine automotive keys. In addition, counterfeit keys do not have the proper markings required by most vehiclesâ€™ security systems.


Are you considering purchasing an aftermarket car key from a China-based company? Then be warned that there is a big chance it will not work. These companies do not accept returned keys that have been cut. That means you will be the one who ends up out of pocket. Likely, you will be responsible for paying the auto locksmith fees for cutting and programming it. Imagine spending money on the key itself and then needing to spend more on a new one.

Buying Used and Locked Up Aftermarket Car Key Replacement

When buying a used car key online, you might not be able to use it on the car you intended. Most vendors do not mention that a second-hand key is usually locked to the first vehicle it was programmed for. Once programmed to a car, a key becomes locked to that vehicle and cannot be reused or reprogrammed for others. Sadly, many customers still buy aftermarket keys online that are incompatible with the vehicles they were intended for! So do not let yourself be one of them!
